Manifesto (written by the same person. I'm >95% sure):

Good art is about the current milieu. If good art is about the past or future, it must be mediated by the current moment. Good art speaks to the present. The current moment is intense, chaotic, bizarre – ‘light’ or ‘modernist’ or ‘minimalist’ art feels dated because it is not about our current moment. It doesn’t say anything, and it doesn’t disrupt. It falls within the conventions of the art world.

Good art is now.

To be political is to expose the state of the world as it is (not masking). It is either anti- or post-. Disrupting has to be confrontational.

...

Rich people go to the museum as a pleasurable cultural experience. Woke rich people (creative elite) go to the relational aesthetics piece and have soup.
