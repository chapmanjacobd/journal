Fidelity's Solo 401 (k) plan is pretty flexible because they allow After-tax contributions: https://www.fidelity.com/viewpoints/retirement/401k-contributions

It's a little confusing at first but after a bit of reading and maths you'll find it is far more flexible than a SEP-IRA. But it requires a bit more bookkeeping--keep track of contributions and you'll need to submit a form yearly to the IRS after the retirement plan is worth more than $250k
