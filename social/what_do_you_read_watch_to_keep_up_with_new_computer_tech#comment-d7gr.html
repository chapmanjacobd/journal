It's only gotten easier to be build PCs but it isn't really cheaper anymore. There are some deals to be had and some not so good deals with both.

But choosing the best value or best performance parts, not just compatible parts, has gotten a little bit more difficult because there are either too many options or too few options. Simple example: you probably don't need a 1000w PSU and it will almost certainly be less efficient than a 500w PSU for smaller workloads.

CPU Interconnect, FCLK, etc has always been a thing but I'd argue that it's even more important nowadays to pay attention to nowadays. Single thread speed has kind of plateaued and any frequency mismatches add up quickly:
