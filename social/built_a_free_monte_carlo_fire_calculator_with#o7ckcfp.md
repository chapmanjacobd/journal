It uses [GBM](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Geometric_Brownian_motion) to model sequence of returns risk 

You can adjust the volatility to model previous timeframes, for example:

| Regime              | 20-Year Window | μ (Real Return) | σ (Volatility) |
|---------------------|----------------|-----------------|----------------|
| Early 20th Century  | 1900–1920      | 4–6%            | 18–22%         |
| Great Depression    | 1929–1949      | 0–2%            | 25–35%         |
| Post-War Boom       | 1949–1969      | 7–9%            | 12–15%         |
| Stagflation Era     | 1965–1985      | 0–3%            | 15–20%         |
| Great Moderation    | 1980–2000      | 8–10%           | 13–17%         |
| Dot-com + GFC Era   | 2000–2020      | 4–6%            | 15–20%         |
| Long-Run Average    | 1900–2020      | 6–7%            | 15–18%         |
